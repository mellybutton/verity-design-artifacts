How BECAUSE works
The only field only you can author.
IFThe condition — when does this rule apply?
THENThe action — what should the rep do?
BECAUSEThe reasoning — why this rule exists. Only a human can write this. AI never generates it.
1
BECAUSE is your voice, not a summary
Don't explain what the rule does — explain why it matters. What failure have you seen? What did the data show? What would a new rep get wrong without this?
2
The contrastive trick
If you're stuck, ask yourself: what would go wrong if this rule didn't exist? That answer is almost always a stronger BECAUSE than a direct explanation.
3
One domain, one rule, your name
Every BECAUSE is attributed to you. Your team — and the AI — will see it. This is the mechanism by which your expertise gets preserved instead of lost in handoffs.
Example BECAUSE
Rule: IF renewal ≤30d AND health <60 → schedule EBR
"EBR creates mutual accountability — the customer names a success owner, which changes the dynamic from vendor-push to shared commitment. Without this, we were winning renewals on price and losing relationships. The EBR is the relationship checkpoint, not the renewal checkpoint."
— Jordan Chen · Renewal domain
Why this matters
Rules without BECAUSE can't be trusted by the AI layer. They're structurally incomplete. A BECAUSE is what turns an observed pattern into a governed decision. Your judgment is the signal.
