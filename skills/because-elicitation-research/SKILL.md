# because-elicitation-research — v1

Weekly research + ideation ritual: how to keep BECAUSE field reasoning quality high as practitioners become familiar with Verity over time.

**Triggers on:** "run the BECAUSE research", "what does the research say about keeping humans sharp", "ideation on elicitation quality", "run the weekly BECAUSE skill", "what's new on cognitive friction"

Every session: (1) searches relevant research domains → (2) one concrete UI/design idea per finding → (3) updates Notion idea library.

---

## Problem statement

As Jordan (CS Principal) uses Verity repeatedly, the novelty of the BECAUSE prompt fades. Familiarity breeds automation — practitioners start filling in faster and shallower. The risk: the rule library's quality degrades at exactly the moment the organization trusts it most.

This skill tracks research on how to prevent this. It is a narrow, cumulative, design-generative ritual — not a general research digest.

---

## Research domains to search (each session, rotate emphasis)

1. Cognitive load theory — when does scaffolding reduce thinking vs. enable it?
2. Deliberate practice — how to sustain effortful engagement in repeated tasks
3. Accountability and ownership — what makes people write for future readers vs. just themselves
4. Friction design — strategic resistance that preserves reasoning quality
5. Peer review / code review psychology — analogous structured judgment tasks
6. Self-explanation effect (Chi et al.) — generating reasoning improves retention
7. Reflection prompts in learning design — what makes someone actually pause
8. Automation complacency literature — trust calibration in repeated AI+human loops

---

## Session format

### 1. Research pull (web search)
Search 2–3 domains. For each finding:
- Summarize the finding in 1–2 sentences (paraphrase, no direct quotes)
- Extract the design implication: "This suggests that..."
- Generate one concrete UI/interaction idea for the BECAUSE field that applies this finding

### 2. Idea output
Each idea must have:
- **Name** (short, memorable)
- **Mechanism** (what it does, one sentence)
- **Design sketch** (described in words or rendered as an artifact)
- **Pros / cons** (2 each)
- **Confidence** (Low / Medium / High — based on evidence strength)

### 3. Notion update
Save each idea to the Elicitation Lab gallery (DB: `f2cdce94-4b22-48aa-94a3-de602bccdfc6`). Fields: Name, Date, Domain, Mechanism, Confidence, Status (Idea / Prototyped / Tested / Rejected).

---

## Guardrails

- Ideas must be grounded in a research finding — no pure speculation
- Never propose anything that auto-fills or AI-generates BECAUSE content
- Never propose anything that reduces the confirmation requirement
- Ideas that increase friction are welcome; ideas that bypass human judgment are not
- If a finding contradicts prior work in the library, flag the contradiction explicitly
